Personas Dataset Upgrade (personas_v0 → personas_v1)
1. Overview

The personas dataset was upgraded from personas_v0.csv to personas_v1.csv to improve realism, diversity, and applicability for simulation and eligibility analysis.

The previous version focused only on barrier-related attributes. The updated version introduces demographic and socio-economic dimensions, making the dataset suitable for both:

access risk simulation
eligibility discovery
2. Limitations of personas_v0

The initial dataset had the following limitations:

Limited attributes (only barrier-related fields)
No demographic information (age, gender, location)
No eligibility-relevant attributes (occupation, income, student status)
Low diversity in population representation
Weak differentiation between personas

As a result, the dataset was insufficient for modeling real-world population behavior.

3. Objectives of personas_v1

The upgraded dataset was designed with the following objectives:

Represent realistic population segments in India
Enable eligibility matching for welfare schemes
Improve variation in barrier impact simulation
Ensure logical consistency across attributes
Introduce diversity across socio-economic groups
4. Updated Schema

The new dataset includes the following fields:

Demographic Attributes
persona_id
age
gender
rural_urban
Socio-economic Attributes
income_level
occupation
education_level
Eligibility-related Flags
student_status
farmer_status
disability_status
Barrier-related Attributes
literacy_level
digital_access
document_completeness
institutional_dependency
5. Design Methodology

The dataset was constructed using a structured approach:

Step 1 — Category Definition

Personas were grouped into categories such as:

rural vulnerable populations
urban low-income groups
students
farmers
stable urban populations
special groups (elderly, disabled)
Step 2 — Base Persona Creation

Representative base personas were created for each category.

Step 3 — Controlled Variation

Additional personas were generated by varying:

age
income level
education
occupation

This ensured coverage without randomness.

6. Consistency Rules

To maintain realism, the following rules were applied:

Graduate education implies high literacy
No formal education implies low literacy
Elderly individuals (age > 60) have limited or no digital access
Rural low-income personas do not have full digital access (except rare cases)
Student occupation implies student_status = yes
Farmer occupation implies farmer_status = yes
Higher income correlates with higher document completeness
7. Diversity and Coverage

The dataset includes:

balanced rural and urban representation
a wide age distribution (youth, middle-aged, elderly)
multiple occupations (farmer, student, worker, unemployed, self-employed)
representation of vulnerable groups (disabled, elderly)
variation in digital access and documentation availability
8. Improvements Over Previous Version

The upgraded dataset provides:

improved realism and logical consistency
better simulation variability
support for eligibility-based filtering
inclusion of edge cases and special groups
enhanced distribution of document completeness values
9. Final Outcome

The final personas_v1.csv dataset contains:

25–30 personas
structured and consistent attribute values
strong diversity across population segments

This dataset is now suitable for:

access risk modeling
policy intervention simulation
eligibility discovery integration
10. Conclusion

The upgrade from personas_v0 to personas_v1 transforms the dataset from a simple simulation input into a structured representation of real-world population diversity.

This forms a critical foundation for building the Persona Mapping Function and enabling realistic system behavior.
