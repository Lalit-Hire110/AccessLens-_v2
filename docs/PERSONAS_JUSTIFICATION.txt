# Synthetic Personas Justification – AccessLens v2

## Purpose of the Personas Dataset

The synthetic personas dataset in AccessLens v2 represents hypothetical but realistic
access situations faced by individuals when interacting with government welfare schemes.
These personas are not real people and do not correspond to any identifiable individual
or group.

The purpose of personas is to:
- Illustrate how different combinations of access barriers affect outcomes
- Enable controlled simulation of access risk
- Test the sensitivity of schemes and barriers under diverse conditions

Personas act as *inputs to the access risk model*, not as predictions of real-world behavior.

---

## Design Principles

The personas dataset is designed using the following principles:

1. **Synthetic and Non-identifiable**  
   All personas are fictional and constructed solely for simulation purposes. No real
   personal data is used, stored, or inferred.

2. **Barrier-Oriented Design**  
   Each persona represents a distinct configuration of exposure to awareness, digital,
   documentation, and institutional barriers.

3. **Interpretability**  
   Attributes are expressed using coarse, human-readable categories rather than numeric
   precision. This ensures that simulation outcomes can be easily explained to non-technical
   audiences.

4. **Coverage of Extremes and Intermediates**  
   The dataset includes:
   - High-access personas with minimal friction
   - Low-access personas with multiple compounding barriers
   - Intermediate cases that reflect common real-world situations

---

## Persona Attributes

Each persona is described using five attributes:

- **literacy_level**  
  Indicates general reading and comprehension ability relevant to understanding scheme
  information and forms.

- **digital_access**  
  Represents access to internet-enabled devices and ability to use digital platforms.

- **document_completeness**  
  Reflects availability and readiness of required documents at the time of application.

- **institutional_dependency**  
  Indicates reliance on intermediaries, officials, or local institutions to navigate the
  access process.

These attributes are intentionally high-level and do not encode demographic, economic,
or sensitive characteristics.

---

## Ethical and Methodological Notes

- Personas do not imply eligibility or entitlement.
- Personas are not used for profiling, targeting, or automated decision-making.
- All persona attributes are illustrative and used solely to explore access dynamics.
- The dataset supports scenario analysis and policy reasoning, not individual assessment.

This approach ensures that AccessLens v2 remains privacy-preserving, transparent, and
ethically grounded.
