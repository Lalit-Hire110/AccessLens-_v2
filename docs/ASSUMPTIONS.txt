DATA GAPS AND LIMITATIONS – AccessLens v2

1. Application mode ambiguity
Some schemes, such as entitlement-based health schemes, do not have a clearly defined
national-level application process. In such cases, application_mode is left blank to reflect
the absence of explicit enrollment procedures.

2. Document count variability
Official guidelines often list multiple document variants or allow substitutions. Document
counts are therefore approximate and represent distinct document categories rather than
state-specific variations.

3. State-level process differences
Several schemes are implemented by states with localized portals, forms, and verification
procedures. National-level datasets may not capture these differences uniformly.

4. Steps count uncertainty
The number of application or verification steps is not consistently documented in official
sources. Where step counts are included, they are conservative approximations derived from
form structure or process descriptions.

5. Assisted access heterogeneity
While assisted access mechanisms (e.g., Anganwadi workers, CSCs, scheme facilitators) are
acknowledged, their availability and effectiveness vary across regions and are not modeled
quantitatively.

6. Digital infrastructure variability
Digital access barriers depend on local connectivity, device availability, and portal
performance. These factors are represented qualitatively through personas rather than
measured directly.

7. Temporal changes
Scheme processes, portals, and documentation requirements may change over time. The dataset
reflects the state of information at the time of collection and is not continuously updated.

8. Absence of empirical outcome data
AccessLens v2 does not use real enrollment, rejection, or transaction data. As a result, the
model does not validate outcomes against real-world success or failure rates.
